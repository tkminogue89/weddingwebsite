How it started

Our Story

Why Joshua Tree?

Nate's from Indiana. Teri's from Jersey. They live in New York City. You may be asking yourself why they want you to travel all the way to California for their wedding.

Click here for the whole story

Back in January 2023, not too far along into their relationship, Teri and some friends decided to spend a month in San Diego. With the idea of a month apart simply unbearable, Teri invited Nate to join for a quick visit and, thus, their first scheme was born. They quickly put together an overly-involved spreadsheet of every possible iteration a Californian road trip could take — Mountains? Beaches? Lakes? Desert?

The first stop? Joshua Tree National Park.

Teri and Nate bonded over their love of travel, exploration, and overpriced grilled cheese sandwiches from greasy diners. It was on that trip that they truly realized just how compatible they are — and that maybe this was truly something special. They were tested on the trip, experiencing nearly every emotion together:

  • Sadnessfinding out the allergen-friendly bakery Nate found for Teri was bad
  • Excitementthe general store has s'mores PopTarts!
  • Fearrealizing the remote dirt road with no cell service we had driven on for the last 1.5 hours was impassable
  • Happinesseven the long, boring desert roads didn't faze them because they were together
  • Concernopen-air bathrooms 🫠
  • Hopethere is a spark that neither had experienced before

Since that Californian road trip, they have gone on to many other schemes — campervanning in New Zealand, safari-ing in Kenya, snorkeling in Bermuda, hiking in the Azores. But there's something special about the very first scheme. When thinking about where to have their wedding, they knew they wanted something special to them (another scheme!). And that's how they landed on Joshua Tree.

If you're reading this, you've played a role in our story and we're so appreciative of you. While we recognize it's a lot to ask, and completely understand that not everyone will be able to make it, we hope you're able to join us as we begin our marriage in a place where, in many respects, we feel like it all started.

What to wear

Dress Code

Desert formal

Think golden hour in the desert: warm earthy tones, breathable linen, and a layer for when the temperature drops after dark.

See our inspiration board

Typical early May in Joshua Tree

~86°F Sunny days
~57°F Cool nights

Warm, sunny, and very dry by day; the high desert cools off quickly once the sun sets — so a light layer for the evening is perfect.

Consider this inspiration, not instruction. Above all, we want you comfortable — wear something you already love and feel great in. Weddings are expensive enough without a shopping spree on our account, so please don't feel you need anything new. Just come as you, ready to dance in the desert.

FAQ

Can I bring a plus-one?

Your invitation will name everyone we've saved a seat for. If it says "and guest," absolutely bring someone.

Are kids welcome?

We love your little ones, but this one's an adults-only evening so you can let your hair down.

What's the weather like?

Early May in Joshua Tree means warm, sunny days and cool desert nights — it can drop quite a bit after sunset, so bring a layer. Sunscreen and water are always smart in the desert.
